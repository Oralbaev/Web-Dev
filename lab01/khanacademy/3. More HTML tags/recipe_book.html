Spaghetti Carbonara

  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Spaghetti 400g
Pancetta or bacon 200g
Eggs 4
Grated Parmesan cheese 100g
Black pepper To taste

Steps:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in boiling salted water until al dente. Drain, reserving a cup of pasta water.
  2. Cut the pancetta or bacon into small pieces and fry until crispy.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, Parmesan cheese, and black pepper.
  4. Add the cooked spaghetti to the pan with the pancetta. Remove from heat and quickly toss with the egg mixture, adding a little reserved pasta water if needed to create a creamy sauce.
  5. Serve immediately, garnished with extra Parmesan cheese and black pepper.

Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

  • Time: 25 minutes
  • Makes: 24 cookies
Ingredients Quantity
All-purpose flour 2 1/4 cups
Butter, softened 1 cup (2 sticks)
Granulated sugar 3/4 cup
Brown sugar, packed 3/4 cup
Eggs 2
Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon
Baking soda 1 teaspoon
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Semi-sweet chocolate chips 2 cups

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add to the creamed mixture and mix well.
  5. Stir in chocolate chips.
  6. Drop dough by rounded tablespoonfuls onto prepared baking sheets.
  7. Bake for 9-11 minutes or until golden brown. Cool on wire racks.

Vegetarian Chickpea Curry

  • Time: 40 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Chickpeas (cooked or canned) 2 cups
Onion, chopped 1 large
Garlic cloves, minced 2
Ginger, grated 1 tablespoon
Tomatoes, chopped 2 medium
Coconut milk 1 can (400ml)
Curry powder 2 tablespoons
Turmeric powder 1 teaspoon
Cayenne pepper (optional) To taste
Salt To taste
Vegetable oil 2 tablespoons

Steps:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add chopped onions and cook until softened.
  2. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, curry powder, and turmeric powder. Cook for 2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they break down and become saucy.
  4. Stir in coconut milk and chickpeas. Season with salt and cayenne pepper (if using). Simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Serve hot with rice or naan bread.
